# One Vote

- Source family: NAEP / NCES
- Assessment year: 1998 writing assessment (released scoring guide publication 2000)
- Grade: Grade 12
- Form: persuasive response
- Cohort coherence: same grade, same prompt, same NAEP rubric family, six ordered exemplar responses
- Timing note: NAEP 1998 released prompts were generally 25-minute responses

## Prompt

Your school is sponsoring a voter registration drive for 18-year-old high school students. You and three of your friends are talking about the project. Friend 2 says that people should vote as soon as they turn 18 because it is one of the responsibilities of living in a democracy. Friend 3 says there is no point because one vote will not change anything. Do you agree with friend 2 or 3? Write a response to your friends in which you explain whether you will or will not register to vote. Support your position with examples from your reading or experience and try to convince the friend with whom you disagree.
