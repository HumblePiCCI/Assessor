# Rubric

NAEP focused holistic scoring uses a six-level ordered scale.

## 1. Unsatisfactory
- Attempts a response but may only paraphrase the prompt or be extremely brief.
- No control over organization.
- No control over sentence formation; inaccurate word choice across the response.
- Grammar, spelling, and mechanics errors severely impede understanding.

## 2. Insufficient
- Fragmentary, highly repetitive, or very undeveloped response.
- Very disorganized or too brief to detect organization.
- Little control over sentence boundaries and formation; inaccurate word choice in much of the response.
- Errors make understanding very difficult in much of the response.

## 3. Uneven
- Attempts a complete response but is partial, list-like, disjointed, or underdeveloped.
- Weak progression of ideas/events; parts may not fit together cleanly.
- Uneven control over sentence boundaries and word choice.
- Errors sometimes interfere with understanding.

## 4. Sufficient
- Clear complete response with limited development and few details.
- Ideas/events are generally related, though with few transitions or some inconsistency.
- Simple but mostly controlled sentences and word choice.
- Errors generally do not interfere with understanding.

## 5. Skillful
- Clear response with development in parts and some relevant descriptive/supporting detail.
- Clearly organized, though transitions or continuity may lapse.
- Some variety in sentence structure and some specific/good word choice.
- Errors do not interfere with understanding.

## 6. Excellent
- Well-developed response with relevant detail across the response.
- Well connected and well organized with transitions.
- Varied sentence structure and specific/precise word choice.
- Strong control of mechanics; errors are few and do not interfere with understanding.
