High schools should start later because the current early schedule reduces learning and well-being. A later start time would not solve every problem, but it would align school with students' ability to focus and would likely improve the quality of learning time.

First, tired students do not learn as effectively. When students arrive exhausted, they struggle to concentrate and they often feel less motivated. Even if a lesson is well planned, a student who is half-asleep cannot engage fully. A later start gives students more sleep, which can translate into better attention, improved memory, and stronger participation.

Second, an early start adds stress to the morning routine. Many students juggle buses, siblings, or long commutes. When everything is rushed, students begin the day anxious. Starting later provides a buffer that can lower stress and improve mood, which matters for classroom behavior and relationships.
