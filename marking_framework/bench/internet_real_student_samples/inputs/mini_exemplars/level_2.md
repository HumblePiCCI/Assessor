Schools should start later for teenagers because sleep affects learning and mental health. Starting early might seem normal, but it does not match how many students actually function in the morning.

When school starts very early, students often arrive tired. Being tired makes it harder to pay attention, remember information, and participate. A later start would give students more time to sleep, which could improve focus in class. It could also reduce stress, because students would not feel rushed every morning.

Some people argue that a later start would interfere with after-school activities and jobs. This is a real concern, but schedules can be adjusted. Sports practices could begin later, or some activities could move to mornings for students who prefer that. Schools already adjust schedules for events, so they can adjust for start times too.
