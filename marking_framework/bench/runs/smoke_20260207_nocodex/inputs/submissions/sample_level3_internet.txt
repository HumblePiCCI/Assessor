Dear Principal,

I am writing to recommend a school-wide waste reduction plan. Our school community is large, and even small daily habits create a lot of garbage by the end of each week.

My first recommendation is to place clearly labeled recycling and compost bins in high-traffic areas. Right now, students often use the closest bin, even when it is the wrong one. Better labels and placement would improve sorting accuracy.

My second recommendation is to run a short advisory lesson each month on responsible disposal. Students are more likely to follow routines when they understand why the routines matter.

My third recommendation is to track results publicly. If classes can see weekly waste totals, they can set goals and feel proud when they improve.

These changes are practical, affordable, and educational. They would make our school cleaner while helping students build lifelong environmental habits.

Sincerely,
Student
