MAPLE STREET - City council approved a new protected bike lane on Maple Street Tuesday night, voting 7-2 in favor of the project. The lane will run from 3rd Avenue to 12th Avenue, with construction expected to begin in the spring.

Council member Dana Brooks said the lane is a response to safety concerns. "We have had too many close calls on Maple Street," Brooks said. The plan includes a barrier separating cyclists from vehicle traffic and updated signage at key intersections.

Not all residents agreed. Some supporters argued the lane will encourage active transportation and reduce conflicts between cars and bikes. Others raised concerns about traffic congestion and the loss of street parking. Local business owner Mark Ellis said the change could affect customers. "Parking is already tight on weekends," Ellis said.

City staff said they will monitor traffic and safety after installation and report back to council within six months. The project is part of a broader transportation plan focused on safer streets for drivers, cyclists, and pedestrians.
