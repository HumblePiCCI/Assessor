MAPLE STREET - City council approved a new protected bike lane on Maple Street Tuesday night, voting 7-2 in favor of the project. The lane will run between 3rd Avenue and 12th Avenue, and construction is expected to begin in the spring, according to city staff.

Council member Dana Brooks said the project responds to ongoing safety concerns. "We have had too many close calls on Maple Street," Brooks said, pointing to the street's high traffic volume and limited space for cyclists. The design includes a physical barrier between bikes and vehicles, updated lane markings, and new signage at several intersections.

The decision drew mixed reactions from residents and business owners. Supporters said protected lanes can reduce conflict by giving cyclists dedicated space and by making traffic patterns more predictable. Critics focused on practical impacts, including reduced curbside parking and potential congestion. Local business owner Mark Ellis said the change could affect customers. "Parking is already tight on weekends," Ellis said.

City staff said they will review traffic flow and safety outcomes after the lane is installed and will provide an update to council within six months. The Maple Street project is part of a broader transportation plan aimed at improving safety for drivers, cyclists, and pedestrians.
