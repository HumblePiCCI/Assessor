Dear Principal,

I am writing to propose a targeted environmental initiative: a three-stream waste system paired with student-led monitoring. Our school has enough student traffic that modest behaviour changes can produce visible, measurable impact within one term.

First, we should adopt standardized recycling, compost, and landfill stations at predictable locations. In many schools, contamination happens not because students refuse to participate, but because the disposal choices are inconsistent from one area to another. Consistent stations reduce decision fatigue and improve accuracy.

Second, we should train a rotating student eco-team to conduct brief weekly audits. Their role would not be punitive. Instead, they would identify problem areas, share quick reminders, and celebrate improvements. This creates accountability while preserving a positive school culture.

Third, we should publish simple monthly metrics for the community. When progress is visible, participation rises because students can connect daily actions to concrete outcomes.

This plan combines infrastructure, education, and accountability. It is realistic within school operations and would strengthen both environmental practice and student leadership.

Sincerely,
