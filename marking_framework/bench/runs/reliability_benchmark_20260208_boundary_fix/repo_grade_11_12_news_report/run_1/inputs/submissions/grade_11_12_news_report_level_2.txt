City council voted Tuesday night to approve a new protected bike lane on Maple Street. The lane will run between 3rd Avenue and 12th Avenue and construction is expected to begin in the spring.

Council member Dana Brooks said the project is meant to improve safety for cyclists. "We have had too many close calls on Maple Street," Brooks said. The lane will include barriers separating bikes from vehicle traffic.

Some residents supported the plan, while others worried about parking and congestion. Local business owner Mark Ellis said he is concerned about fewer parking spaces. The city said it will review traffic patterns after the lane is installed.

The project is part of a larger transportation plan that aims to make streets safer for all users.
