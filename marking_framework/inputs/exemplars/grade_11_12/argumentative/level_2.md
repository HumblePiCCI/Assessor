AI writing tools should be allowed in school if students disclose how they used them. These tools can support learning, but they can also be misused if there are no expectations.

One reason to allow AI tools is accessibility. Some students struggle with organizing ideas, grammar, or confidence. An AI tool can help a student get started and can suggest ways to improve clarity. This does not automatically replace thinking, but it can reduce barriers.

Another reason is relevance. AI tools exist outside of school, and students will encounter them in workplaces. Schools can teach responsible use instead of pretending the tools are not available. For example, teachers can require students to show drafts or to explain their choices.

The concern is that students will submit AI work as their own. This is a real issue. However, the solution should be better rules, not a total ban. If students must disclose AI use and complete parts of the work in class, teachers can reduce misuse.

Overall, allowing AI with clear disclosure supports learning and prepares students for reality, while still protecting academic honesty.
