RIVERSIDE - A weekend clean-up at Riverside Park brought together students, families, and staff to remove litter and improve the public space. The event, organized by the school eco-club, ran from 10:00 to 11:00 a.m. on Saturday and targeted the playground area and walking path.

Volunteers collected wrappers, bottles, and paper while also sorting recyclables. Grade 7 student Riley Chen said the amount of litter was surprising. "I did not realize how much garbage was hidden in the grass," Chen said. By the end of the hour, the group had filled multiple large bags and cleared trash from several corners of the park.

Ms. Patel, the eco-club advisor, said the clean-up was designed to support both the community and the environment. "A clean park is safer for kids, and it also protects animals that might mistake trash for food," she said. Patel added that the club hopes to build better habits by showing how quickly litter adds up.

Some volunteers suggested adding more garbage bins near the playground. Others said reminder signs could encourage visitors to take their trash with them. The eco-club plans to discuss next steps at its next meeting and will consider another clean-up in the spring.
