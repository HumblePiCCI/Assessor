RIVERSIDE - Students, families, and teachers gathered at Riverside Park on Saturday to take part in a community clean-up. The event began at 10:00 a.m. and focused on litter along the walking path and playground.

Volunteers wore gloves and carried garbage bags as they collected wrappers, bottles, and paper. "I did not realize how much garbage was hidden in the grass," said Grade 7 student Riley Chen. By the end of the hour, the group had filled several large bags and separated recyclable items.

The clean-up was organized by the school eco-club to encourage environmental responsibility. Ms. Patel, a teacher advisor, said the group wanted to make the space safer for children and wildlife. "When trash is left behind, animals can get hurt and the park becomes less welcoming," she said.

The eco-club plans to host another clean-up in the spring and is considering adding reminder signs near the entrances. For now, volunteers said they hope the cleaner park will motivate visitors to use the garbage bins.
