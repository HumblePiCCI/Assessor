RIVERSIDE - Riverside Park looked noticeably different by late Saturday morning after a school-led clean-up removed litter from the playground and surrounding paths. The one-hour event, organized by the school eco-club, ran from 10:00 to 11:00 a.m. and drew students, families, and staff.

With gloves, grabbers, and garbage bags, volunteers combed the grass and trail edges for wrappers, bottles, and paper. "I did not realize how much garbage was hidden in the grass," said Grade 7 student Riley Chen, who joined the clean-up with a parent. The group separated recyclables and filled several large bags of waste.

Ms. Patel, the eco-club advisor, said the goal was both practical and educational. "A clean park is safer for kids, and it also protects animals that might mistake trash for food," she said. Patel explained that the club is trying to connect everyday choices to real consequences in shared spaces.

Community members also discussed prevention. Some volunteers noted that bins near the playground overflow on busy days, while others suggested reminder signs at entrances. The eco-club plans to review feedback and consider follow-up actions, including another clean-up in the spring. For now, organizers hope the visible results will encourage park visitors to use bins and keep the area clean.
