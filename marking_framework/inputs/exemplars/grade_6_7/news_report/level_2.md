Students and families helped clean up Riverside Park on Saturday morning. The event started at 10:00 a.m. and lasted about one hour. Volunteers picked up litter, sorted recycling, and filled many bags.

"I did not realize how much garbage was hidden in the grass," said Grade 7 student Riley Chen. The group found plastic wrappers, bottles, and old paper. Some volunteers also pulled weeds near the walking path.

The clean-up was organized by the school eco-club. Teacher Ms. Patel said the goal was to make the park safer and to remind people not to litter. The club plans to put up reminder posters next month.

After the clean-up, the park looked cleaner and more people were using the playground. Volunteers said they felt proud of the difference they made.
