Pollinators are animals that help plants reproduce by moving pollen between flowers. Common pollinators include bees, butterflies, moths, and even some birds. Without pollinators, many plants would not be able to make seeds and fruit.

How pollination works
When a pollinator visits a flower to drink nectar, pollen grains stick to its body. As the pollinator moves to another flower, some of that pollen transfers. This helps the plant create fruit and seeds. Pollination supports foods we eat, like berries, squash, and apples.

Why pollinators need help
Pollinators can struggle when there are not enough flowers, when habitats are removed, or when harmful sprays are used. If a neighborhood has mostly grass and few flowering plants, pollinators have less food. If chemicals are sprayed on blooming flowers, pollinators can be harmed.

How people can help
People can plant a mix of native flowers and let some areas grow naturally. It helps to choose plants that bloom at different times so pollinators have food in spring, summer, and fall. Another helpful step is avoiding chemical sprays, especially on flowers. Finally, you can provide shallow water with stones so insects can land safely.

Conclusion
Pollinators play an important role in gardens and ecosystems. By planting more flowers and being careful with chemicals, people can make neighborhoods safer for pollinators.
