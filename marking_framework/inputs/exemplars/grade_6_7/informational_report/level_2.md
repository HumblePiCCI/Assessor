Pollinators are animals that move pollen from one flower to another. Bees, butterflies, and some birds are pollinators. Pollination helps plants make seeds and fruit, which means it helps humans and animals too.

One way pollinators help gardens is by visiting many flowers. When a bee lands on a flower, pollen sticks to its body. Then the bee flies to a different flower and some pollen falls off. This helps the plant grow fruits like apples or berries.

Pollinators are having a hard time in some places because there are fewer flowers and because of chemicals. People can help by planting a variety of flowers. It is also good to have flowers that bloom at different times, like spring and summer, so pollinators have food longer.

Another way to help is to avoid spraying chemicals on flowers. If you must use something, follow the directions and never spray when bees are active. You can also leave a small water dish with stones so insects can land.

In conclusion, pollinators are important because they help plants reproduce, and small actions can help protect them.
