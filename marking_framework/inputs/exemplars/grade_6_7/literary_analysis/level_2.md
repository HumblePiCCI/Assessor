In the short story "The Last Lap," Maya wants to win so badly that she makes a risky choice. The theme is that choices have consequences, and those consequences can help you grow.

At the start, Maya is focused on beating everyone. She says, "All I could hear was my own breathing and the word win." This shows she is thinking more about winning than about running smart. When she tries to cut in too fast, she falls. The consequence is immediate: she loses time and feels embarrassed. The line, "The track scraped my palms like sandpaper," shows how the mistake hurts for real, not just in her feelings.

After the fall, Maya has to decide what kind of person she will be. She could blame the other runner, but she admits, "I hated the sound of my own excuses." That shows she is learning, even if she is still upset.

Overall, the story shows that mistakes can teach you if you actually listen to them.
