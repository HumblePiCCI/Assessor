In "The Last Lap," Maya learns that one careless decision can change everything. The author shows that consequences are not only punishments, they are lessons that can shape who you become.

First, Maya's mistake happens because she is thinking about winning more than running responsibly. Early in the story she says, "All I could hear was my own breathing and the word win." This line shows her focus is narrow. When she tries to cut in quickly, she falls. The author makes the consequence physical: "The track scraped my palms like sandpaper." Maya cannot ignore the result of her choice because she feels it.

Second, the consequence forces Maya to be honest with herself. After she falls, she wants to blame someone else, but she admits, "I hated the sound of my own excuses." This evidence shows Maya recognizes she is making excuses. The consequence is uncomfortable, but it pushes her to take responsibility instead of staying stuck.

Finally, Maya starts to change. She does not magically become perfect, but she begins to think about what matters. When she says, "Next time, I will run my race, not their race," it shows she learned the lesson. She still wants to win, but she also wants to do it the right way.

Overall, the story teaches that consequences can hurt, but they can also help you grow if you face them honestly.
