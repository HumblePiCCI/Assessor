NORTHVIEW - Northview Secondary School's gym transformed into a showcase of student research on Thursday afternoon as the annual science fair brought together Grades 8 to 10 projects, families, and community judges. Displays highlighted both experimentation and engineering, with students explaining their work to a steady stream of visitors.

Among the projects was Grade 9 student Amina Hassan's investigation comparing soil types and plant growth. "I wanted to test which soil helped plants grow faster," Hassan said, adding that she tracked changes over time and recorded observations to support her conclusion. Other students demonstrated model bridges, filtration systems, and data-based inquiries into everyday questions.

Teacher advisor Mr. Lewis said the science fair is designed to build skills that do not always show up in regular assignments. "Students learn how to ask a question, gather evidence, and communicate results clearly," he said. Judges assessed projects for clarity, creativity, and how well students connected evidence to conclusions.

The event ended with certificates and small prizes for top projects, and several displays will be featured in the school library next week. Organizers said they hope the fair encourages more students to participate next year, not only to compete, but to practice curiosity and clear communication.
