NORTHVIEW - Student-designed experiments and engineering displays filled the Northview Secondary School gym on Thursday as the school hosted its annual science fair. The event brought together students in Grades 8 to 10, along with families and community volunteers who served as judges.

Projects included model bridges, water filtration systems, and investigations into plant growth. Grade 9 student Amina Hassan said her project compared soil types and measured changes over time. "I wanted to test which soil helped plants grow faster," Hassan said, explaining that she tracked growth patterns and recorded observations in a log.

Teacher advisor Mr. Lewis said the fair helps students develop research and communication skills. "Students learn how to plan an investigation, analyze results, and explain what they found," he said. Judges evaluated projects based on clarity, creativity, evidence, and presentation.

At the end of the afternoon, winners received certificates and small prizes. Organizers also selected several projects to be displayed in the school library next week so other students can view them. The school hopes the event will continue to grow and inspire future participants.
