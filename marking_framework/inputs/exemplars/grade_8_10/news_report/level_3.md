NORTHVIEW - Northview Secondary School held its annual science fair on Thursday afternoon, filling the gym with student projects and visitors. The event featured experiments, engineering builds, and research displays created by students in Grades 8 to 10.

Projects ranged from model bridges to water filtration systems and plant growth experiments. Grade 9 student Amina Hassan said her project compared different soil types. "I wanted to test which soil helped plants grow faster," Hassan said. Students explained their methods, results, and conclusions to judges and community members.

According to teacher advisor Mr. Lewis, the science fair is designed to build academic skills beyond a test. "Students learn how to plan an investigation and then communicate what they found," he said. Judges evaluated projects based on clarity, creativity, and evidence.

Winners received certificates and small prizes, and several projects will be displayed in the library next week. Organizers said the school hopes the fair will encourage more students to participate next year.
