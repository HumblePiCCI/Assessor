Sleep is important for teenagers because it affects learning, mood, and health. Many teens feel tired during school days because their schedules are busy and they stay up late.

When teens do not sleep enough, it can be harder to focus in class. They may forget information more easily and feel less motivated. Lack of sleep can also affect emotions. Some teens feel more stressed or irritated when they are tired.

There are a few habits that can support better sleep. One habit is having a consistent bedtime and wake-up time, even on weekends. Another habit is reducing screen time before bed, because scrolling can keep the brain active. It can also help to prepare for sleep with a routine, like reading or listening to calm music.

Finally, the sleep environment matters. A dark, quiet room helps, and keeping the phone away from the bed can reduce distraction. Overall, better sleep supports better days at school.
